FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreigners own property in Cambodia?
+
Foreigners can own condominiums (strata-title units) from the 1st floor upward with up to 70% of a building. Land ownership requires a Cambodian co-owner or a long-term lease structure. Our advisors will guide you through the most suitable approach.
What is the typical rental yield in Phnom Penh?
+
Prime areas such as BKK1 and Daun Penh typically yield between 6–9% annually, outperforming many Asian markets. Pre-build investments can offer even higher returns upon completion.
Do you provide bilingual (Japanese/English) support?
+
Yes. All of our advisors are bilingual in Japanese and English, and we provide full documentation and communication in both languages from search to contract signing.
How long does it take to complete a rental contract?
+
For rental properties, contracts are typically finalized within 1–2 weeks of finding the right unit. For purchases, the process takes 4–8 weeks including due diligence and legal review.
Are there property management services available?
+
We partner with trusted property management firms and can handle tenant sourcing, rent collection, maintenance coordination, and annual reporting on your behalf.
Can I view properties remotely before visiting?
+
Absolutely. We offer virtual tours, video walkthroughs, and detailed photo reports, allowing overseas clients to shortlist properties before their first visit to Phnom Penh.
